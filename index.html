Introduction to Magic in Baldur's Gate 3

Spell Schools

Spells in Baldur's Gate 3 are categorized into eight distinct schools of magic:

  • Abjuration
  • Conjuration
  • Divination
  • Enchantment
  • Evocation
  • Illusion
  • Necromancy
  • Transmutation
school = spell.get_school()

Each school focuses on a specific type of magical effect, granting spellcasters unique capabilities and specialized strategies.

Spell Casting Mechanics

To cast a spell in Baldur's Gate 3, a spellcaster must:

  • Have the spell prepared or known
  • Have available spell slots of the appropriate level
  • Spend the required action (action, bonus action, or reaction)
  • Succeed on any required ability checks or saving throws

The specific mechanics vary depending on the spellcasting class and the spell being cast.

Cantrips

Cantrips are minor spells that spellcasters can cast at will, without expending any spell slots. These magical effects are often used for utility, defense, or small-scale damage. Some examples of cantrips in Baldur's Gate 3 include:

  • Mage Hand
  • Prestidigitation
  • Light
  • Guidance
  • Toll the Dead
cantrip.cast() // No spell slot required

Leveled Spells

In addition to cantrips, spellcasters in Baldur's Gate 3 have access to a wide array of leveled spells. These more powerful magical effects require the expenditure of spell slots and are categorized by spell level, ranging from 1st to 9th. Some examples of leveled spells include:

  • Fireball (3rd level)
  • Invisibility (2nd level)
  • Revivify (3rd level)
  • Counterspell (3rd level)
  • Polymorph (4th level)
spell.cast(spell_slot_level)

Spell Slots and Rest

Spellcasters in Baldur's Gate 3 have a limited number of spell slots available, which they use to cast leveled spells. These spell slots are replenished through a short or long rest, depending on the spellcasting class.

  • Short Rest: Regains all expended spell slots of 1st level and 2nd level.
  • Long Rest: Regains all expended spell slots.

Concentration

Many spells in Baldur's Gate 3 require the caster to maintain concentration in order to sustain their magical effects. If the caster takes damage or is incapacitated, they must make a Constitution saving throw or lose concentration on the spell.

  • Only one concentration spell can be active at a time
  • Certain effects can cause a loss of concentration
  • Losing concentration ends the spell effect immediately

Magic Items

The world of Baldur's Gate 3 is rich with magical items that grant powerful abilities to the user. These items come in various forms, such as weapons, armor, and accessories. Some examples of magical items include:

  • Cloak of Protection
  • Wand of Magic Missiles
  • Boots of Speed

Legendary Spells

Legendary spells are incredibly powerful and rare spells that only a few spellcasters in Baldur's Gate 3 can access. These spells often have massive effects on the battlefield or the story's narrative. Some examples of legendary spells include:

  • Time Stop
  • Wish
  • True Resurrection

Arcane Vs Divine

There are two main types of spellcasting in Baldur's Gate 3: arcane and divine magic. Arcane magic is typically associated with wizards, sorcerers, and warlocks, while divine magic is often wielded by clerics and paladins. The key differences include:

  • Arcane spells are learned and manipulated by the caster
  • Divine spells are granted by deities or higher powers
